In-home Observation for Zorro

October 2008 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Ipsos.

Role: Ethnographic Researcher

Techniques: In-home observation & In-depth interviews

Zorro Deliverables







About Zorro

Zorro is an Argentine brand with products leading the laundry process.

Project goals

Main goal was to gain insights about how stay-at-home mothers approach and execute their laundry process. The secondary goal included an understanding of the emotional aspects involved in identifying clean and dirty clothing.

Process: methods and techniques

We requested the client previous qualitative and quantitative reports to review the existing information. I worked together with another anthropologist to conduct in-home interviews and observe women practicing their laundry process. We interviewed, observed and video-recorded four women. We reached out during the study to ascertain the representations and significations associated with laundry, clothing cleanliness, psychological impacts, relational connections. We delivered a detailed presentation as well as eight video summaries of the video-recorded interviews and investigations. We curated the video content to bring out the most valuable insights.

Highlights:

  • Clean clothing speaks about your identity and competency as a wife, as a mother and someone responsible for your family's well-being.
  • The need for clothing to be washed goes beyond seeing the clothing as dirty. The passing of the time requires washing it.
  • The clothing that has one stain is not necessarily seen as dirty however it requires washing. This means that usually much more clothing is being washed than what advertising promotes for its products.
