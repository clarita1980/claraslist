La Salteña Shopper

August 2010 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Role: Ethnographic Researcher

Techniques: Observation in-store with casual interviews

La Salteña Deliverables

About La Salteña

La Salteña is an Argentine well-known brand. The brand portfolio includes pastas and dough for empanadas and pies.

Project goals

The main goal of the research was to identify key decision drivers when purchasing pastas and dough as well as provide information on the brand image and insights for improving the communication in-store.

Process: methods and techniques

This research was performed on team with two other anthropologists. We started with an internal desk research to analyse the information that already existed within the company. We did observations of men and women purchasing pasta or dough (what they do) in supermarkets and mini-markets as well as casual interviews to understand their thinking (what they say).

Highlights:

  • Shoppers compared pastas to foods such as hamburgers and hot dogs.
  • Ingredients like meat and chicken do not have a good reputation inside the pastas since it is associated to processed meat.
  • Shoppers identified a gap in the market offering referring to wheat dough and pastas made with seeds.
  • There are some difficulties with La Salteña pasta packaging. Users have hard time finding and recognizing the ingredients.
  • When out of the pocket moments, users tend to buy one package of a premium brand like La Salteña and and the other from a cheaper brand.
  • There is a positive reaction towards the brand when discussing the dough category and not so positive when relating the brand to the pasta category.
