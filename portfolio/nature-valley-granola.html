Nature Valley Granola Shopper

June 2010 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Role: Ethnographic Researcher

Techniques: Observation in-store with casual interviews

Nature Valley Deliverable

About Nature Valley

Nature Valley belongs to General Mills corporation brand portfolio. The brand has a variety of granola, protein and nut bars products.

Project goals

Nature Valley wanted to perform an exploratory research about Cereal Bars consumers to understand their needs and desires. As a secondary goal they wanted to understand the behavior of cereal bar buyers in-store and prioritize the drivers that influence the final decision.

Process: methods and techniques

We started with an internal desk research to gather and analyse existing and available information and start from that point. This research was performed on team with two other anthropologists. We interviewed in-store men and women buyers of the brand as well as from the competitors brands. We video recorded the buying process and once the purchase decision was made we showed the video to the interviewee to go step-by-step understanding and analysing the drivers in the decision.

(We added a sign to let users know about the presence of the camara)

Highlights:

  • Cereal bars are perceived as a substitute snack for cookies and chocolates between lunch and dinner with a bit more nutrition or less fat.
  • Consumers do not put cereal bars on their supermarket shopping list.
  • Although they are available in large supermarkets, consumers tend to buy them in kiosks on the street.
  • There are opportunities for point-of-purchase events to influence the purchase.
  • Cereal bars are consumed on-the-go and mostly with soda.
  • One important purchase driver is the texture of the bar: soft and humid vs. dry and hard.
  • With new ingredients like chocolate or yogurt the cereal bars are seen as the healthy version of candies.
