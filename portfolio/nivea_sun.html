Nivea Sun Shopper

January 2010 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Role: Ethnographic Researcher

Techniques: Observation in-store with casual interviews

Nivea Sun Deliverables

About Nivea

Nivea is a global skin- and body-care brand owned by the German company Beiersdorf.

Project goals

In 2008 I was hired to perform a shopper study for Nivea Facial and Body Creams. In 2010 I was hired again to prepare a shopper study for Nivea Sun and compare results.

The goal of the research was to understand the behavior, motivators and pain points of Sunscreen buyers when purchasing a product.

Process: methods and techniques

The research was performed on team with two other anthropologists. We started with an internal desk research to analyse the information that already existed within the company. We observed customer behaviors and pain points that surfaced during the purchase process and performed casual interviews in-store with men and women buyers of Nivea sunscreen as well as buyers from competitor brands. Once done with the research we prepared an ethnographic report with findings and recommendations for the category and the brand and presented to the client.

Highlights:

  • In the minds of consumers the concept of the sun generates ambivalent ideas: 1. They want to be tan but not as much as to be seen like a dry raisin. 2. They need to take care of their skin but also need to look good.
  • There is a tension between the rational side of the brain, which suggests care and protection against UV rays, and the emotional side, which seeks corporal beauty and joy via links to a tanned body.
  • Consumers feel disoriented at the moment of purchasing one product because of over-specific product variants. Product offering variants are based on skin type, skin color and solar protective factor. For example: Deramglos sunscreen factor 60 is also advertised for people with extra sensitive skin but this confused some women that do not perceive their skin as sensitive but are willing to protect it from the sun.
  • There are opportunities for point-of-purchase events to influence the purchase.
  • Purchasing a sunscreen is a concern that arises just before the vacations. Few people will use it to walk or exercise outdoors during the rest of the year.
  • Each part of the body requires a different treatment and therefore a different factor and/or brand.
